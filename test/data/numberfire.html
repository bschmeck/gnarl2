NFL PLAYER NEWS

J. Mixon RB

Joe Mixon is third on the Bengals' running back depth chart

Cincinnati Bengals running back Joe Mixon is third on the team's Week 1 depth chart.

What It Means:

Mixon is listed behind fellow backs Jeremy Hill and Giovani Bernard, essentially the same order the trio has been during the preseason. Hill will get the majority of the carries while Bernard and Mixon will mix in on passing downs and as a change of pace versus the Baltimore Ravens in Week 1.

Our models project Mixon for 60.1 total yards and 0.3 touchdowns on 12.8 touches for 9.3 FanDuel points in Week 1. He carries a price tag of $6,400 versus the Ravens.

Source: ESPN
K. Benjamin WR

Cam Newton says Panthers' Kelvin Benjamin will have his best season in 2017

Carolina Panthers quarterback Cam Newton said that 2017 will be wide receiver Kelvin Benjamin's best season yet.

What It Means:

It's been a rough go of it for Benjamin in 2017. He has been forced to deal with some weight issues and the passing of his mother back in July. However, Benjamin came in at a lean 243 pounds at camp and has since dedicated his season to his mother. If he can remain healthy, he should be Newton's primary target out wide.

With a Week 1 projection of 4.3 receptions, 63.8 yards and 0.33 touchdowns on 7.6 targets, Benjamin's 10.5 FanDuel points would rank 26th among wideouts. At $6,500, he's not on par with elite receiver options but he is in a plus matchup against the San Francisco 49ers' questionable pass defense.

Source: Charlotte Observer
G. Kittle TE

George Kittle named 49ers' starting tight end in Week 1

San Francisco 49ers tight end George Kittle is listed as the starting tight end for Week 1 against the Carolina Panthers.

What It Means:

Following Vance McDonald's trade to the Pittsburgh Steelers last month, Kittle figures to see the majority of snaps at tight end for the 49ers in 2017. After Pierre Garcon, there isn't a lot of talent in terms of pass-catchers on San Francisco's roster, so the fifth-round rookie out of Iowa could be worth a look as a streaming option in Week 1, as well as a possible add in deeper formats.

numberFire's models project Kittle to score 3.57 FanDuel points at a cost of $4,500 in Week 1. He's the TE32 in our weekly fantasy rankings with a projection of 1.8 catches for 18.8 yards and 0.14 touchdowns.

Source: Matt Barrows
T. Hill WR

Tyreek Hill to be 'heavily included' in Chiefs' Week 1 gameplan

Kansas City Chiefs wide receiver Tyreek Hill is expected to be "heavily included" in the offensive gameplan against the New England Patriots on Thursday Night Football, according to Steve Wyche of NFL Network.

What It Means:

Per Wyche, Hill isn't expected to be used as a return man on special teams, but he could see snaps in the slot and split out wide, as well as out of the backfield. Rookie running back Kareem Hunt still figures to serve a workhorse role for the Chiefs, but head coach Andy Reid wants to maximize Hill's exceptional speed and athleticism by using him all over the field. The fifth-round sophomore will open the year as a high-end WR2 with all types of upside.

numberFire's models project Hill to catch 4.8 passes for 51.4 yards and 0.42 touchdowns in Week 1. He's tied for WR29 in our weekly rankings and is expected to score 10.13 FanDuel points at a cost of $6,700.

Source: Steve Wyche, NFL Network
J. Brown WR

Jaron Brown listed as Arizona's number-two wideout

Arizona Cardinals wide receiver Jaron Brown is listed as the team's second receiver in their Week 1 depth chart.

What It Means:

Jaron Brown started in the third preseason game and will continue to be opposite Larry Fitzgerald, in two-wide sets to start the year. He will likely rotate in and out with John Brown and J.J. Nelson, much as head coach Bruce Arians did during the preseason. Even with the depth chart listing, the 27-year-old isn't expected to see enough targets to make him relevant in anything but 14-plus team leagues.

Our models project Jaron Brown for just 0.2 receptions and 3.4 yards for 0.6 FanDuel points in Week 1.

Source: Mike Jurekci, ArizonaSports.com
J. Brown WR

John Brown listed with Cardinals' second-team offense

Arizona Cardinals wide receiver John Brown is listed behind Jaron Brown on the team's Week 1 depth chart.

What It Means:

Jaron Brown will start opposite of Larry Fitzgerald in two-wideout sets in Week 1 but expect John Brown to see plenty of time on the field as head coach Bruce Arians will continue to rotate players alongside Fitz, much as he had during the preseason. John Brown was limited with a quad injury this preseason but finished strong with a two-touchdown performance in the fourth and final preseason game.

Our models project John Brown for 3.4 catches, 51.8 yards, and 0.3 touchdowns for 8.5 FanDuel points versus the Detroit Lions in Week 1.

Source: Mike Jurecki, ArizonaSports.com
